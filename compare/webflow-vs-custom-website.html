// honest breakdown

The Honest Breakdown

Webflow
What it's good at
  • Stunning visual editor — drag-and-drop with pixel-level control and no hand-coding required
  • Clean, production-quality exported code that developers actually respect
  • Built-in CMS for blog-heavy or content-rich sites where editors update copy constantly
  • Solid native SEO controls — meta, OG, canonical, and sitemap all configurable in the UI
  • Strong ecosystem of templates and third-party integrations for rapid prototyping
  • Great choice for designers and agencies that need to deliver multiple client sites
Webflow
Where it falls short
  • Steep learning curve — despite the "no-code" marketing, most non-designers struggle significantly with the editor
  • Monthly subscription runs $168–$468 per year, indefinitely, even if your site never changes
  • Platform lock-in — your site lives on Webflow's servers; leaving means rebuilding from scratch
  • Overkill for simple business sites that just need a hero, services section, and a contact form
  • Hosting and CMS tiers can get expensive fast as traffic or team size grows
// custom build advantage

Where a Custom Build Wins

Cost that actually makes sense long-term. Three years on Webflow's Basic plan runs you at minimum $504. Three years on the CMS plan — the one most small businesses actually need — is $1,404. A custom-built site hosted on GitHub Pages costs you zero dollars per month in hosting. You own the domain outright (~$12/year), and the code lives in your GitHub repo forever. There's no subscription to cancel, no pricing tier to outgrow, and no invoice waiting for you every month whether you changed anything or not.

No platform lock-in, ever. When your site lives in a Webflow account, leaving means rebuilding it somewhere else from scratch. That's leverage Webflow holds over every customer. A custom-built site is just files — HTML, CSS, JavaScript — sitting in a GitHub repo that you own. You can move it to any host, hand it to any developer, or run it locally tomorrow. The work you paid for stays yours, unconditionally.

SEO foundations without the guesswork. Every custom build includes a sitemap.xml, canonical tags, Open Graph metadata, and hand-written meta descriptions for every page — not boilerplate defaults. These are the exact signals search engines use to understand and rank your site. Unlike template-based platforms where defaults are often wrong or generic, a custom build gets these right from day one because someone who cares about your specific business wrote them.

Personal service, not a ticket queue. When you work with Robby, you're not filing a support ticket with a faceless platform. You get direct communication, two full rounds of revisions, and a 7-day post-launch support window to catch anything that needs fixing. For small business owners who just want their site to look great and work correctly — without becoming a Webflow expert — that direct relationship is worth more than any visual editor.

// who it's for

Who Should Choose Each

Webflow

Choose Webflow if...

  • You're a designer or developer who wants to build and tweak your own site without touching code
  • You run a marketing or content team that needs non-technical editors updating layouts weekly
  • You're an agency building multiple client sites and need a repeatable visual workflow
  • Your site needs a robust visual CMS with custom content collections and complex filtering
Custom Build

Choose a custom build if...

  • You're a small business owner, freelancer, or side hustler who wants it built and launched — not taught
  • You're tired of paying monthly platform fees for a site that rarely needs layout changes
  • You want a professional online presence with real SEO foundations, not defaults nobody checked
  • You want to own your site outright, with no platform holding your content hostage
// verdict

The Bottom Line

Webflow is a genuinely impressive tool — if you're the kind of person who will actually use it. For designers, agencies, and teams who live inside a visual editor, it earns its monthly fee. But for most small business owners and freelancers, Webflow is overkill: you'll spend days learning a platform just to produce a site you could have had in two weeks without touching a single setting. A custom build makes more financial and practical sense when you want a high-quality result, real SEO from day one, zero monthly fees, and a site you own completely — with someone available to handle the work so you don't have to.
