# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Developers, founders, freelancers, and small business owners who find Robby through YouTube content, organic search (free tools, competitor comparisons, blog posts), or direct referral. They arrive with a specific need — AI automation advice, a website built for them, or clarity on where to start with AI — and they evaluate Robby's credibility primarily through the site itself. The site is the first deliverable they see; if it looks amateurish, the pitch is already lost.

Secondary audience: anyone Googling "Squarespace alternatives," "website cost estimator," or "AI automation quiz" — discovery visitors who arrive cold and need to be converted to believers without a sales call.

## Product Purpose

A personal brand site for Robby J — AI engineer, content creator (YouTube: @Code-With-Robby), and service provider. The site does three jobs simultaneously:

1. **Convert** — turn visitors into website build or AI consultation inquiries
2. **Establish credibility** — the craft of the site is a live portfolio for the Website Build service
3. **Generate organic traffic** — free tools, competitor comparison pages, and blog posts target high-intent searches

Success looks like: a prospect landing on the site, immediately trusting Robby's competence, finding the service that fits their situation, and emailing to book.

## Brand Personality

Clean, credible, professional. The tone is direct and confident — an engineer who ships, not a hype-man. Robby speaks plainly and shows the work. No "AI is changing everything" filler. The site should feel like it was built by someone who knows exactly what they're doing, because that IS the pitch.

Three-word anchor: **precise · direct · earned**.

## References

**Linear.app** — the target aesthetic register. Extremely tight dark UI, sharp typography, precise spacing, understated premium. Engineering credibility communicated through restraint, not decoration. Nothing there to impress; everything there to work. That discipline is the aspiration: the site should feel like it was made with the same care Robby brings to client work.

## Anti-references

- **Generic AI startup landing pages** — floating 3D orbs, purple gradients on everything, "The future of work is here" hero copy, glow effects on every card. The current site trends toward this register and should be steered away from it. More restraint, less neon.
- **Boring corporate portfolio** — stock photos, safe light theme, Times New Roman-adjacent fonts, zero personality. The opposite failure mode: looks like it was made in 2011 and hasn't been touched since.
- **Fiverr/Upwork freelancer profile energy** — transactional, price-first, race to the bottom. The site should position Robby as a specialist worth paying for, not a commodity.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the portfolio.** Every page is a live demonstration of what Robby delivers to clients. Sloppy spacing, inconsistent type, or low-contrast text on any page undercuts the Website Build pitch before a word is read.

2. **Restraint is the credibility signal.** A glow on every card is visual noise. One deliberate accent — used sparingly — lands harder than decoration everywhere. When in doubt, do less. Linear uses almost no color and reads as premium; the decorative instinct is the failure mode to resist.

3. **Show the work, skip the hype.** Real client outcomes, real tools, real build quality do the persuasion. Copy should be concrete and specific ("7-day post-launch support window," "1–2 week turnaround") not vague and ambitious ("transforming businesses with AI").

4. **Precision earns trust.** Tight spacing, sharp alignment, correct contrast ratios, and consistent micro-details are not perfectionism — they're proof of craft. A prospect deciding whether to pay for a website build is watching closely.

5. **Dark, not flashy.** The dark theme is legitimate: developers and AI-tool users work in dark environments, and dark says "serious" not "trendy." But dark + neon glow soup reads as AI hype tool, not trusted specialist. Keep the dark; earn the color.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA as the floor. Body text (`--text` #F5F0E8 on `--bg` #0C0B08) measures 17.5:1 — maintain it. `--text-muted` (#9B9480 on #0C0B08) measures 6.50:1 — passes AA at all text sizes. `--amber` (#F59E0B) on dark bg measures 9.0:1. **Amber-background elements** (buttons, gradient badges, avatars) use `color: var(--bg)` for dark text — white text fails on amber (1.9:1). No contrast risk in the current palette.

No known specific user needs beyond standard web accessibility. Reduced motion support required for any animations added to the site.
