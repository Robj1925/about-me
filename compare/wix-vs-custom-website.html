Wix: The Full Picture

✓ What Wix is good at
  • Enormous template library — hundreds of designs across every niche
  • Drag-and-drop editor lets non-technical people publish without writing a line of code
  • Built-in app marketplace for forms, bookings, and e-commerce add-ons
  • AI website builder can generate a rough draft site in minutes
  • Hosting, SSL, and infrastructure handled for you automatically
✕ Where it falls short
  • Notoriously poor SEO — Wix sites consistently rank below comparable custom builds
  • Bloated JavaScript and render-blocking code drag down Core Web Vitals scores
  • You can't switch templates without rebuilding your entire site from scratch
  • Monthly subscription fees never stop — $200–$420/yr, every year, indefinitely
  • Free plan shows Wix ads on your website, which looks unprofessional
  • Sites can feel generic — visitors can often tell it's a Wix site at a glance
// the real difference

Where a Custom Build Actually Wins

SEO is where the gap is widest. Wix generates bloated HTML with heavy JavaScript that search engine crawlers struggle to process efficiently. A custom-coded site delivers clean, semantic markup — proper heading hierarchy, schema-ready structure, canonical tags, a working sitemap.xml, and Open Graph tags out of the box. These aren't nice-to-haves; they are the basic table stakes for Google to understand and rank your pages. Starting from a Wix template means you're starting with a handicap Google can measure.

Page speed is an SEO signal, and Wix struggles with it. Google's Core Web Vitals directly affect how your site ranks. Wix loads a significant amount of platform overhead on every page — their editor runtime, analytics scripts, and app marketplace code — even for visitors who never interact with any of it. A custom build ships only the code your site actually needs, which translates to faster load times and better scores in Google Search Console.

The cost math is straightforward over time. Wix costs $17–$35 per month, which adds up to $204–$420 per year, every year, indefinitely. Three years in, you've spent $600–$1,260 on hosting alone — and you still don't own anything. A custom build is a one-time project fee plus roughly $10–15 per year for your own domain name. After year two, you're ahead financially regardless of the project cost. Free GitHub Pages hosting is fast, reliable, and backed by Microsoft infrastructure.

Design quality and personal service are harder to quantify but easy to notice. Wix templates are professionally made, but they're used by millions of businesses. A custom dark or light theme built around your brand doesn't look like anyone else's website — because it isn't. And instead of figuring out Wix's editor on your own, you get a real person who handles the build, writes the copy, does two rounds of revisions, and stays available for a week after launch. That's a fundamentally different experience than a DIY platform.

// decision guide

Who Should Choose Each

Choose Wix if...

  • You're in early exploration mode and just want to see if an idea has legs — SEO doesn't matter yet
  • You need to update text, images, or products yourself every single day without involving anyone else
  • You need built-in e-commerce or bookings without any development work
  • You're a hobbyist building a personal page with no business goals tied to organic traffic

Choose a custom build if...

  • You're a small business owner or freelancer who needs Google to actually find you
  • You want a professional site that doesn't look like a template and doesn't cost $35/month forever
  • You want someone to handle the whole thing — design, copy, launch — without you learning a platform
  • You're tired of paying Wix and want to own your online presence outright
// verdict

The Bottom Line

Wix is a genuinely useful product for getting something online fast — if organic traffic isn't part of your plan and you need to edit content yourself every week. But for small business owners and freelancers who need Google to find them, Wix's SEO limitations, slow load times, and never-ending subscription fees are real costs that compound over time. A custom-built site starts with better SEO foundations, loads faster, looks uniquely yours, and costs a fraction of the long-term Wix bill. If your website is supposed to generate leads or build credibility, custom is the smarter investment.
