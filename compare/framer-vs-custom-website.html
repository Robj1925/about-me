The Honest Breakdown

Framer — What it's good at

  • Best-in-class animation and motion interactions
  • Blazing fast page loads with clean code output
  • Gorgeous modern aesthetic out of the box
  • Great for design portfolios and SaaS landing pages
  • Visual editor that designers already know how to use
  • No developer required if you're design-savvy

Where it falls short

  • Monthly subscription — $180 to $360 per year, forever
  • Platform is still maturing; features change without notice
  • CMS is not as capable as WordPress or a custom solution
  • Template library is narrow and skews toward design portfolios
  • Not ideal if you're not already comfortable in design tools
  • Locked into Framer's hosting and publishing pipeline
// the case for custom

Where a Custom Build Wins

Cost compounds in Framer's favor — until it doesn't. At $15–$30 per month, Framer's subscription looks small on day one. But after two years you've paid $360–$720 and you still don't own anything. A custom-built site has a one-time cost, is hosted free on GitHub Pages, and you keep full ownership. That's money that stays in your pocket every single year.

SEO foundations that actually hold up. Framer gives you meta fields and decent page speed, but a truly custom build gives you granular control: hand-tuned meta descriptions, canonical tags, a sitemap.xml, Open Graph images, structured heading hierarchies, and clean semantic HTML that search engines reward. These aren't big-ticket features — they're table stakes for ranking, and every one is included in a custom build from day one.

Design that fits your brand, not a template's aesthetic. Framer's templates are stunning — if you're a digital designer or SaaS startup. For a local service business, a consultant, or a freelancer, those motion-heavy templates can feel like wearing someone else's suit. A custom build starts from your brand, your audience, and your actual goals. Dark or light theme, exactly the sections you need, copywriting that speaks to your customers — nothing bolted on, nothing missing.

You don't have to touch the tech. One of Framer's selling points is that designers can build without code. But most small business owners are not designers either. With a custom build, Robby handles everything: the design, the copy, the mobile responsiveness, the SEO setup, and the deployment. You review, request changes, and go live. The optional monthly support plan means you never have to log into a CMS or figure out where to upload an image.

// pick your path

Who Should Choose Each

Choose Framer if...

  • You're a designer or creative studio who wants to own the build process
  • Your brand identity depends on high-end motion and interaction design
  • You're launching a SaaS product and want a polished landing page fast
  • You're already comfortable with visual design tools and don't mind the subscription

Choose a custom build if...

  • You're a freelancer, small business owner, or side hustler who just needs a solid site
  • You don't want to pay a monthly platform fee forever
  • You want someone to handle design, copy, and setup end-to-end
  • You need a clean, fast, professional presence — not a portfolio showpiece

The Bottom Line

Framer is a genuinely impressive platform — if you're a designer who wants to build and maintain your own site and you're okay paying monthly for it. For the average small business owner or freelancer, though, it's more tool than you need and more cost than you should carry. A custom-built site gives you a cleaner path: one-time investment, no platform dependency, full SEO control, and a real human who handles it for you. If Framer's animations are core to your brand identity, use Framer. If your goal is a fast, professional, low-overhead web presence that generates leads — a custom build wins on every practical dimension.
