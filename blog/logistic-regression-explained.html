Logistic Regression: Why It’s Not Actually 'Regression'

Think Logistic Regression is for predicting numbers? Think again! Join me as I explain how this simple AI tool helps computers make 'Yes' or 'No' decisions.

📅 December 2, 2025 · ⏱ 2 min read

Is it Regression? Nope!

Hi there! I’m Robby. As an AI engineer, I see people get confused by this all the time. When you hear the word "regression" in math, you usually think about predicting a number—like the price of a house or the temperature tomorrow.

But here is the secret: Logistic Regression is actually used for classification. It doesn’t tell you how much; it tells you what kind.

Making the Right Choice

Think of Logistic Regression as a super-fast decision-maker. It takes a bunch of data and calculates the probability of something being a "Yes" or a "No."

It’s like asking the computer: "Is this true?" The computer looks at the data and gives you a score. If the score is high enough, the computer says "Yes!" If it’s low, it says "No."

Where Do We Use This?

You probably use this technology every single day without knowing it. Here are two real-world examples:

  • Spam Filters: When your email decides if a message is "Spam" or "Not Spam," it’s using this tool!
  • Customer Predictions: Companies use it to guess if a customer will buy a product or if they might leave.

Why It’s Awesome

As an engineer, I love Logistic Regression because it’s simple, fast, and very smart. It helps us organize the world into buckets of "Yes" and "No" so that we can make better decisions.

Next time you hear someone say "Logistic Regression," just remember: it’s all about sorting things into the right box!
