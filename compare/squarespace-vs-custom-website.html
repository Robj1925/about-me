Squarespace — The Full Picture

What it's good at

  • Beautiful, polished templates that look professional out of the box
  • All-in-one platform — hosting, domain, and builder in one bill
  • Easy drag-and-drop editing — no developer needed after launch
  • Built-in e-commerce with payment processing
  • Strong customer support and a stable, well-documented product

Where it falls short

  • Monthly subscription that never ends — $192 to $588 every year, indefinitely
  • Limited SEO control — you can't touch the underlying HTML, and Squarespace ships a lot of JavaScript that slows your pages down
  • Template constraints — customization stops at what the template allows
  • Your site looks like every other Squarespace site — hard to stand out in search or on first impression
  • Slow Core Web Vitals scores, which Google factors into rankings
// the case for custom

Where a Custom Build Actually Wins

The cost difference compounds fast. Squarespace's cheapest plan runs $192/year — and the plan most small businesses actually need (with more pages, advanced SEO, and no Squarespace branding) runs $276 to $588. After just two years, you've spent over $1,000 and you still don't own anything. A custom build has a one-time cost. Your only ongoing expense is a domain name, which runs about $10–15 per year. You can own your domain outright, host it for free on GitHub Pages, and never write another subscription check.

SEO is where the gap is largest. Squarespace gives you a meta description box and a page title field. That's the extent of your SEO control. A custom-built site gives you full access to every SEO lever — structured canonical tags, a proper sitemap.xml, Open Graph tags for social sharing, meta descriptions per page, and clean HTML that search engines can actually read. On top of that, custom sites typically load much faster than Squarespace pages because there's no platform layer shipping 300kb of JavaScript before your content appears. Page speed is a direct Google ranking factor, and a lean custom build wins here by a wide margin.

Your design will actually be yours. Every Squarespace template has a signature look — and even with customization, visitors who browse the web regularly can spot a Squarespace site instantly. A custom build starts from your brand, not from a template library shared by hundreds of thousands of other businesses. That matters for first impressions, for trust, and for standing out in search results where Google's Page Experience signals increasingly reward fast, well-structured sites over cookie-cutter ones.

You get a real person, not a support ticket. When you work with Robby J, there's no "contact support" form and no 48-hour response window. You get direct communication throughout the build — from the initial conversation about what you need, through two rounds of revisions, to a 7-day post-launch support window where questions get answered the same day. That's not a feature you can buy from Squarespace at any price tier.

// decision guide

Who Should Choose Each

Choose Squarespace if...

  • You want to update your own content every day without asking anyone for help
  • You're running an online store and need built-in payment processing fast
  • You have no budget for an upfront build cost and prefer spreading costs monthly
  • You're testing a business idea and need something live in hours, not days

Choose a custom build if...

  • You're a freelancer, consultant, or small business that needs a professional site and not a DIY project
  • You're tired of paying subscription fees for something you rarely touch after launch
  • You want to rank on Google and need real SEO foundations, not a checkbox
  • You want a site that looks like your brand — not like a template 50,000 other businesses are using
// verdict

The Bottom Line

Squarespace is a solid product for a specific use case: someone who wants to manage their own website daily and needs e-commerce baked in. For everyone else — the freelancer, the local service business, the personal brand, the side hustler — a custom build almost always wins on cost, SEO, and design quality over any realistic time horizon. You pay once, you own it, and you get a site that's actually built to rank. If you don't need to edit your site yourself every week, there's very little reason to rent one from Squarespace indefinitely.
